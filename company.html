Our Story

A decade of effort

We are often glued to our smartphones. Yet, we mostly interact with the smartphone "computer" by looking at a tiny 6-inch screen. The speed of today's human-computer interaction (HCI) is slow, similar to modems of the early 90s. This has bothered Ixana's co-founders for more than a decade. They have worked independently as well as together to make HCI faster, more effective, and more efficient.

The what and why of faster HCI have been clear for years. Faster modems led to Web1.0, fast cellular data led to Web 2.0. Faster HCI would herald new markets. But, how to significantly speed up human-computer interfaces eluded us until recently. Ixana's founder and CTO, Shreyas had an insight that distributed computing could transform HCI. Building on the insight, Ixana has already made significant strides towards faster, more efficient HCI. We are fortunate that a number of team members and investors rallied behind the vision of creating faster, distributed computing HCI similar to the information processing architecture of the human body.
